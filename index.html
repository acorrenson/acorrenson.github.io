Interests

Computer Science is a very wide area of research and every single topic is interesting... However, programming languages theory and formal methods are my favorite topics. More precisely, I'm interested in very practical aspects of the design, implementation and verification of verification tools themselves. So far, I've been essentially working on verified compilers (CompCert) and verified provers (Colibri2). I am also an unconditional fanboy of OCaml (people call me Mr. Caml) and Coq and a great fan of strongly typed functional languages in general.

Teaching and transmitting has always been important for me. I believe that tools such as automated provers or proof assistants could be used to teach mathematics and theoretical computer science to a wider audience. However, such tools are often intended to be used by experts and are therefore very hard to use from a student perspective. I believe (and I hope I'm not completely wrong) that studying practical aspects of the implementation of such systems is a good way to bring them to more people. Providing simple and formally proven implementations of formal verification tools is an amazing way to better understand how and why they actually work!
