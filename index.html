Interests

Computer Science is a very wide area of research and every single topic is interesting... However, programming languages theory and formal methods are my favorite topics. More precisely, I'm interested in automated provers design and verification. I am also a happy user of OCaml and Coq and a great fan of strongly typed functional languages in general.

Teaching and transmitting has always been important for me. I believe that tools such as automated provers or proof assistants could be used to teach mathematics and theoretical computer science in an easier way. However, such tools are often intended to be used by experts and are therefore hard to work with. There is still a lot of research to do to make formal tools easier to use and I find it super exciting!

Projects

I've been working on many projects from various domains of Computer science. All of theme are freely distributed and sources are on my github page. At the moment I'm mainly focused on the formal proof of solvers & tools for automated reasoning. Below are some of the projects I'm currently working on.
  • SATurne is an experiment around the formal verification of a purely functional SAT solver written in Coq
  • Modulus: an SMT solver built from scratch in OCaml by students
  • CQFD: A simple automated prover certified in Why3

Student organizations
